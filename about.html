Our Heritage

Est. 1981, a young tailor named Harry Lahren opened the doors to a modest atelier on London's prestigious Savile Row. Armed with nothing but exceptional skill, unwavering vision, and a deep reverence for British tailoring tradition, he set out to redefine what bespoke craftsmanship could mean in the modern age.

What began as a small workshop dedicated to creating impeccable suits for London's discerning gentlemen has evolved into a global symbol of British refinement and timeless elegance. Yet despite our growth, our core philosophy remains unchanged: every garment we create must honor the heritage of British tailoring while embracing contemporary sensibilities.

We source our fabrics from the finest mills in Italy and Scotland, selecting only materials that meet our exacting standards for quality, durability, and beauty. Each piece is crafted with meticulous attention to detail, from the precision of our hand-stitched buttonholes to the perfect drape of our jacket shoulders. This is not fast fashion—this is clothing designed to become part of your story, to age gracefully, and to remain relevant for decades to come.

Our commitment extends beyond mere aesthetics. We believe in sustainable practices, fair labor, and the preservation of traditional craftsmanship techniques that are rapidly disappearing in our modern world. When you invest in a Harry Lahren garment, you're not just purchasing clothing—you're supporting a legacy of artisanship and becoming part of a tradition that values quality over quantity, permanence over disposability.

Today, Harry Lahren stands as a testament to the enduring power of exceptional craftsmanship. From our flagship store on Savile Row to our boutiques in New York, Tokyo, and Paris, we continue to serve those who appreciate the difference that true quality makes. We invite you to experience the Harry Lahren difference—where heritage meets modernity, and every detail matters.

Est. 1981

The Beginning

Harry Lahren opens his first atelier on Savile Row, a modest 800-square-foot workshop where he personally crafts bespoke suits for London's elite. With just two apprentices and a commitment to perfection, he begins building a reputation for impeccable tailoring and uncompromising quality. Each suit takes over 50 hours to complete, with every stitch placed by hand.

1998

Expansion & Innovation

After years of perfecting the art of bespoke tailoring, Harry Lahren launches the first Ready-to-Wear collection, making the brand's signature quality accessible to a wider audience. This groundbreaking collection maintains the same exacting standards as our bespoke pieces, with each garment undergoing rigorous quality control. The launch is met with critical acclaim and sells out within weeks.

2010

Global Reach

Harry Lahren establishes flagship stores in three of the world's fashion capitals: New York's Fifth Avenue, Tokyo's Ginza district, and Paris's Avenue Montaigne. Each boutique is designed to reflect the brand's British heritage while honoring local architectural traditions. This expansion marks the brand's evolution from a London institution to a global luxury house, yet our commitment to Savile Row craftsmanship remains unwavering.

Today

Modern Classics & Sustainable Future

Harry Lahren continues the legacy with a renewed focus on sustainability and innovation. We've introduced eco-friendly fabrics sourced from certified sustainable mills, implemented zero-waste cutting techniques, and established a garment repair and restoration service to extend the life of every piece. Our latest collections blend cutting-edge fabric technology with traditional craftsmanship, proving that heritage and innovation can coexist beautifully. We remain committed to creating garments that transcend trends and stand the test of time.
